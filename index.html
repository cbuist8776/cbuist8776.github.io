About - Chad Buist

My original path of study in college was at Salisbury University as an Exercise Science Major. So how did I come to switching to a Web Development Major with Full Sail University? Well I was a swimmer growing up and coached for several years after high school and thought that I wanted to do it as a career. Hence the Ex. Sci major. When I went back I started out well but took on too much with being on the swim team, coaching 40 hours a week, and doing college. I eventually got so run down that I had to spend a week in the hospital trying to get my immune system back up. So I took a medical leave from school and was accepted back in the fall but was told that I was on a financial aid probabation which prohibited me financially from going back. So I fell back into my A+ Certification training from high school with computer hardware and software repair and was led eventually into doing SEO and web marketing for businesses using template editors. However I wanted to learn how to build a website myself for my clients instead of outsourcing the work. Then in my search for that knowledge I realized the awesome career potential in the field. So I realized that I needed to go back to college to pick up the skills needed to work in the field. Presently I'm half-way through my degree in Web Development and I hope to later get my Master's in Web Marketing.

Header

Target Audience

The target audience here is mostly for swimmers and coaches looking to advance their skills and knowledge through learning from the video feedback that the site offers. It may be open to others who are not involved in the swimming world who may also be looking to visually learn more and quickly of the sport.

Client and User Needs

The main client and user needs that are satisfied here are the links in the header to joining the website and logging in as members. Secondarily it provides a simple context for the swimmer, coach, or site guest to easily manuver the web site in particular for popular videos and meets that the athletes are interested in.

Critique

The picture of the wave for flo swimming is unique and also universal to those who are involved in the swimming world. Users to the site can immediately recognize that the site is dedicated to the further/betterment of the sport. The blue color of the task bar and the gradient of blue to almost purple represents that the content flows from a simple introduction and can flow to a deeper level of knowledge and comprehension. The tabs being in all caps and yet white in color help the users eyes focus better without feeling too much strain. The header is simple and helps the user "flo" from one aspect of the site to the next.

CTA

Target Audience

The target audience of this Call to Action can be broad. It's color goes along with that of the header in terms of the color choice. So it helps the audience "flo" from one web page to the next. The age demographics here could be a wide variety. However I will say it will appeal to most age-group and college level athletes as well as coaches and or parents

Client and User Needs

The client and user needs here are very simply met. This call to action gives them just enough of an encouragement with the color choice and design to help the client see why they must "Click Here" to move forward.

Critique

As stated earlier the color choice parallels that of the header. The light blue and white can give you the feeling of tranquility that most swimming pools can provide. The white text and blue color without any extra accentuations help the viewer realize that no matter what the next click will take you to it is related most likely to swimming and something that will help benefit you in it.
